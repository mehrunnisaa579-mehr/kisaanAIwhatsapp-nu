# Mango Diseases Management — Punjab / South Punjab

## Purpose

This file is for a FarmAI/KISAAN AI RAG knowledge base. It summarizes mango disease diagnosis and management for Punjab and South Punjab using the uploaded MRS Multan/AARI master directive as the primary source.

---

## 1. Mango Anthracnose / Blossom Blight

### Local names and farmer jargon

- Surkh daagh
- Patton ka jhulsa'o
- Boor ki kungi
- Anthracnose
- اینتھراکنوز
- سرخ داغ
- آم کا جھلساؤ
- بور کا مرجھاؤ

### Pathogen

- *Colletotrichum gloeosporioides*
- *Colletotrichum acutatum*

### High-risk weather and orchard conditions

Anthracnose is highly destructive in South Punjab during monsoon and humid spring windows.

Extreme risk conditions:

- Minimum temperature around **25°C–30°C**
- Relative humidity at or above **90%**
- Active summer rainfall or prolonged wet/humid weather
- Dense, un-pruned canopy
- Fallen leaf litter and infected debris on orchard floor

### Symptoms

#### Leaf stage

- Small irregular dark-brown to black spots on young vegetative flushes
- Spots merge into larger dry brown dead areas
- Leaf margins look scorched or burnt

#### Blossom / boor stage

- Tiny dark lesions appear on floral panicles and floral axis
- Lesions expand and turn the inflorescence black
- Flower drop occurs
- Fruit-set may fail completely in severe attack

#### Fruit stage and post-harvest stage

- Circular sunken deep-black decayed spots on fruit skin
- Rot may develop during transport or ripening
- Pulp may rot under apparently small external lesions

### Action rules for FarmAI

#### Rule 1: Pre-harvest cutoff

Do **not** recommend systemic chemical sprays within **14–21 days of commercial harvesting**. This is important for Maximum Residue Limit (MRL) and export rejection risk.

#### Rule 2: Pruning priority

If the farmer reports a dense un-pruned canopy, prioritize mechanical canopy opening and sanitation before jumping directly to chemicals. Lowering humidity inside the canopy is part of disease control.

### Recommended chemical options from uploaded source

Spray should begin early at protective budburst stage, before boor emergence, and may be repeated at **14-day intervals** if monsoon rains persist.

| Option | Product / Active ingredient | Dose |
|---|---|---|
| A | Nativo 75% WG, Tebuconazole + Trifloxystrobin | **0.9 g per 1 L water** OR **90 g per 100 L water** |
| B | Cabrio Top 60% WDG, Pyraclostrobin + Metiram | **2.50 g per 1 L water** OR **250 g per 100 L water** |
| C | Antracol, Propineb OR Topsin-M, Thiophanate-Methyl | **200–250 g per 100 L water** |

### Non-chemical management

- Prune dense overlapping branches
- Remove fallen infected leaves and debris
- Improve air movement inside the canopy
- Avoid unnecessary humidity buildup
- Remove infected panicles, dead twigs, and diseased material where practical

## 2. Mango Sudden Death / Quick Decline / Dieback

### Local names and farmer jargon

- Aam ka achanak sookhna
- Tanay ka galna
- Gond nikalna
- Sudden Death
- آم کا اچانک سوکھنا
- ڈائی بیک

### Pathogens

- *Lasiodiplodia theobromae*
- *Ceratocystis fimbriata*

### Vector / associated insect

- Mango bark beetle, *Hypocryphalus mangiferae*

The bark beetle damages the wood and may contribute to spread or worsening of the disease complex.

### Symptoms

#### Initial phase

- Darkening of bark
- Thick yellowish-brown gummy material oozes from trunk or branches
- Gummosis visible on main trunk or scaffold branches

#### Acute phase

- Sudden wilting and drying of leaves on one branch
- Drying spreads quickly to wider canopy
- Leaves turn brown but remain attached to dead twigs for months
- Tree may look dry and dead while leaves still hang on branches

#### Internal wood markers

If an infected branch is split open:

- Deep purple streaks may appear
- Dark brown streaks may appear
- Black streaks may appear in internal wood tissue

### Action rules for FarmAI

#### Rule 1: Sanitation and pruning

Immediately prune infected branches **6 inches below visible dead wood margin**.

After pruning:

- Paint the cut surface immediately
- Use Bordeaux paste or Copper Oxychloride paste

#### Rule 2: Trunk and soil drenching

Use direct soil and trunk drenching with:

| Component | Dose |
|---|---|
| Thiophanate-Methyl / Topsin-M | **200 g per 100 L water** |
| Aliette / Fosetyl-Aluminium | **250 g per 100 L water** |

### Mechanical and sanitation controls

- Do not leave infected branches in the orchard
- Remove dead wood quickly
- Treat pruning cuts immediately
- Monitor bark beetle activity
- Isolate severely affected trees for urgent inspection

## 3. Varietal Susceptibility

### Highly vulnerable commercial varieties

- Anwar Ratol
- Chaunsa, including White Chaunsa, Summer Chaunsa, and Black Chaunsa

These are extremely susceptible to:

- Anthracnose / blossom blight
- Mango sudden death

They require high-frequency scouting and defensive pre-monsoon chemical coverage.

### More tolerant / resilient varieties

- Sindhri
- Dusehri

These show better field tolerance against sudden death bark vectors and standard post-harvest skin spotting, but they still require monitoring and sanitation.

## 5. Short Farmer Advice Templates

### Anthracnose response

Your mango tree may have anthracnose if flowers or young leaves have black lesions and the weather is humid or rainy. First open the dense canopy and remove infected debris. If the crop is before harvest safety cutoff, use one of the recommended fungicide options from local guidance, such as Nativo 75% WG at 0.9 g/L or Cabrio Top 60% WDG at 2.5 g/L. Do not apply systemic sprays within 14–21 days of harvest.

### Sudden death response

If gum is coming from the trunk and branches are suddenly drying, it may be mango sudden death or dieback. Prune affected branches 6 inches below dead wood, paint wounds with Bordeaux or copper paste, and drench trunk/soil with Topsin-M 200 g plus Aliette 250 g per 100 L water according to local guidance. Remove infected wood from the orchard.
